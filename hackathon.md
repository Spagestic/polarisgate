# Gemma 4 Hackathon | 2026

## Event Overview

This edition focuses on **Gemma 4** across four core tracks so teams can choose a clear direction and go deep: **Track A - AI Agent** (multi-step planning, tool use, and native function calling for autonomous assistants such as medical pre-screening, legal document workflows, industrial inspection, and education coaching); **Track B - Multimodal** (integrated vision, audio, and language experiences for scenarios like cultural heritage digitization, accessibility tools, and mixed-format document Q&A); **Track C - Edge AI** (fully offline deployment on phones, Raspberry Pi, or embedded hardware with **real device demos**, such as rural healthcare assistants, privacy-preserving local OCR, and low-power agriculture monitoring); and **Track D - AI for Social Good** (solutions for accessibility, climate, public health, education equity, and disaster response). Whether you prefer cloud orchestration, multimodal innovation, edge deployment, or social impact, you can find your main battleground here.

Through rigorous **technical architecture evaluation** and **social impact assessment**, the competition will select 15-30 high-potential projects with strong innovation and execution value. Top teams will be invited to present at the **Google I/O Connect** final demo stage, compete for official recognition, and gain deep technical support and global enablement from the Google ecosystem.

## Track Overview

![AI Agent Track](https://hackathon.googdg.cn/static/image/ai_agent.webp)

### Track A: AI Agent

Build autonomous AI agents with Gemma 4 capabilities in multi-step planning, tool usage, and native function calling.

Examples: medical pre-screening assistant, legal document agent, industrial inspection agent, and education tutoring bot.

![Multimodal Track](https://hackathon.googdg.cn/static/image/multimodal.webp)

### Track B: Multimodal

Deeply integrate vision, audio, and language to deliver truly multimodal experiences beyond simple model chaining.

Examples: cultural heritage digitization, tools for visually impaired users, and mixed-format document Q&A.

![Edge AI Track](https://hackathon.googdg.cn/static/image/edge_ai.webp)

### Track C: Edge AI

Hardcore challenge: fully offline deployment of E2B/E4B-class models on phones, Raspberry Pi, or embedded hardware (real device demo required).

Examples: rural healthcare assistant, privacy-preserving local OCR, and low-power crop disease monitoring.

![AI for Social Good Track](https://hackathon.googdg.cn/static/image/ai_for_social_good.webp)

### Track D: AI for Social Good

Use Gemma 4 to address social challenges such as accessibility, climate, public health, education equity, and disaster response.

Examples: disability assistance tools, climate monitoring, mental health self-help, and humanitarian translation.

## Awards & Judging Criteria

### Award Categories

- **Grand Champion (1)**
  20,000 Cloud Credit, plus GDE nomination opportunity and Google for Startups collaboration opportunities

- **Track Winner Awards (4)**
  Dedicated awards per track

- **Best Mobile App Award (1)** and **Best Edge Deployment Award (1)**

- **Excellence Awards (10)**

### Judging Dimensions

1. **Real-world impact (30%):** Addresses real needs, with clear target users and scalability.
2. **Technical excellence (25%):** Code quality, architecture design, and depth of Gemma 4 usage.
3. **Completeness (20%):** Runnable demo, complete core functionality, and robust edge-case handling.
4. **Innovation (15%):** Novel approach, creative model usage, and unique problem framing.
5. **Presentation quality (10%):** Clear technical report, professional video, and solid code documentation.
